AI Video Production: What It Is and Who's Doing It

AI video is not a gimmick. It's not a replacement for human creativity. It is, however, a genuinely new capability that's changing what's possible — and what's affordable — for brands that move fast. Here's what's actually happening.

By Grid Velocity · June 2026

Two years ago, producing a 30-second brand film took a full crew, a shoot day and a week of post-production. Today, parts of that same workflow can happen in hours — not because AI replaced the creativity, but because it's absorbed the mechanical labour underneath it.

AI video production is still poorly understood — partly because the tools move faster than the conversation about them, and partly because a lot of what gets published is either hype or fear. Neither is useful. Here's what's actually true.

What is AI video production?

AI video production refers to the use of machine-learning models to generate, transform or enhance video content. The category breaks down into three main types:

1. Video-to-video AI (restyle and style transfer)

You provide an existing video. The AI transforms its visual style — changing lighting, colour, texture, mood or even the apparent medium (live action to animation, for example) — while preserving the underlying motion. Tools like Higgsfield excel here. This is one of the fastest and most reliable AI video applications for brands right now.

2. Text-to-video generation

You provide a text prompt; the AI generates video from scratch. The quality here varies enormously depending on the model and the prompt. Results are improving rapidly but remain most useful for short, stylised clips, motion backgrounds, and product showcases rather than complex narrative films.

3. AI-enhanced post-production

This is AI working inside the editing and finishing process — upscaling, frame interpolation, automatic colour matching, AI-driven audio cleanup, automated subtitles and object removal. Most production teams already use some of these tools even without calling it "AI video." Tools like DaVinci Resolve and Adobe have built these capabilities into their standard workflows.

What AI video can actually do for your brand

The honest list of what's genuinely useful right now:

Content volume at low marginal cost. Once you have a core piece of footage or a brand film, AI can help you produce multiple variants — different styles, aspect ratios, moods — without reshooting. For social media content where variety and frequency matter, this is significant.

Fast turnaround on tight briefs. We delivered a detailed video-to-video AI restyle for a client in 2 days — a timeline that would have been impossible with traditional VFX. For time-sensitive campaigns, AI unlocks work that simply couldn't happen before.

Visual styles that don't need a studio. Certain aesthetic looks — painterly, illustrated, cinematic with specific colour treatments — that previously required days of VFX work can now be achieved in a fraction of the time.

Prototyping and pre-visualisation. Before committing to a full production, AI lets you visualise and test a visual direction quickly. This de-risks the creative process significantly.

What AI video cannot do

Being honest about the limitations matters as much as the possibilities:

AI cannot replace a creative director who understands your brand. The model only knows what you tell it — and if the brief is vague, the output will be too. Garbage in, garbage out applies just as much to AI as to any creative process.

AI doesn't understand strategy. A video that's visually impressive but says the wrong thing to the wrong audience fails regardless of how it was made.

Fully AI-generated narrative content — with characters, dialogue and story — still requires significant human intervention to be coherent and on-brand at a professional level.

How Grid Velocity uses AI in video production
