Brand Identity Studio

Branding With
Character.

A brand isn't a logo. It's a world — colours, shapes, words and feeling working together to say something true about who you are. We build those worlds with obsessive attention to craft: designers, storytellers and artists who believe every mark, every shade and every decision should earn its place.

The result isn't just a brand that looks good on launch day. It's one that holds up — across every surface, every campaign, every size — because it was built with conviction from the start.

How We Do It
· Our Process

For a complete brand build, here's how we work. Already have a brand? We meet you where you are.

1

Brand Workshop

We dig into who you are and where you're going. Strategic conversations that surface your positioning, audience and ambition. The foundation everything is built on.

2

Visual Direction

Mood boards and visual direction that nail your aesthetic — colours, type, tone — before a single pixel of logo gets drawn. You choose the path; we execute it to the hilt.

3

Logo & System

The logo is the keystone. We obsess over its shape, weight and movement until it carries your brand at any size, anywhere it lives.

4

Build & Handover

Logo System packaged in every format, Brand Playbook assembled, Launch Kit production-ready. Everything handed over clean, organised and ready to deploy — on day one.

What you get.

• What We Deliver

Every engagement is different. Here's what's in our toolkit — you get what your project actually needs.

Logo System
Not just a logo — the full family. Primary mark, secondary lockup, icon, reversed variants, every format for every surface. Print, digital, social, signage: wherever your brand needs to live.
Brand Playbook
One definitive document your whole team works from. Colour system with print and digital codes, type hierarchy, photography direction, illustration style, voice rules and do/don't examples. No ambiguity, no guesswork.
Launch Kit
Everything needed to go live on day one: social templates, presentation decks, email headers, print-ready files. Not abstract guidelines — production-ready assets you can use immediately.
